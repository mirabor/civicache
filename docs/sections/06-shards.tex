\section{SHARDS Validation}\label{sec:shards}

No exact miss-ratio-curve oracle exists at 1M-access scale --- the classical
$O(n \cdot |\text{unique keys}|)$ exact-stack-distance algorithm would take
hours on the traces in question. We therefore validate our MRC construction
using SHARDS self-convergence (\cite{waldspurger2015shards}): if four
sampling rates produce MRCs that agree with each other at the scale under
test, the sampled MRC is a reliable estimate of the (unavailable) exact MRC.

\subsection*{Methodology}

We follow Phase~4 Plan~04-01's validation design: a $1$M-access synthetic
Zipf trace with $\alpha = 0.8$ and $100{,}000$ unique objects at seed $42$ is
emitted to \texttt{traces/shards\_large.csv} (this trace file is gitignored
per our ${<}5\,$s regeneration recipe). We run SHARDS at four sampling
rates --- $\{0.01\%, 0.1\%, 1\%, 10\%\}$ --- and compare each non-reference
rate against the $10\%$ reference (the highest-fidelity sample in our grid),
computing mean-absolute error (MAE) and maximum absolute error across $100$
aligned MRC points. A companion 50K-access oracle regime with exact stack
distances complements the 1M-scale self-convergence data; that regime applies
a 200-sample floor to drop any rate whose effective sample count would
fall below a statistically meaningful threshold.

\subsection*{Findings}

Three claims follow from the self-convergence data and the complementary
50K-oracle regime:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0pt,topsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{The $1\%$-vs-$10\%$ MAE of $0.0378$ passes the Phase 4
    sanity gate of $<0.05$.} On a higher-skew synthetic than the
    Waldspurger paper's baseline workloads, this is the empirical
    convergence level that motivates trusting any downstream 1M-scale MRC
    we report.
  \item \textbf{At $0.01\%$ sampling, the $81$-effective-sample count falls
    below our $200$-sample floor and is flagged.} We report the rate for
    completeness but recommend against using it as a primary estimate ---
    the max absolute error of $0.25$ (vs.\ $0.067$ at $1\%$) confirms
    point-wise unreliability at this rate.
  \item \textbf{A 50K-access oracle regime with exact stack distances
    cross-validates the self-convergence scaling.} At 50K scale the exact
    MRC is tractable; SHARDS at $1\%$ and $10\%$ reproduces it within the
    same MAE tolerance band, confirming that the convergence behavior
    observed at 1M scale extrapolates from smaller, directly-verifiable
    traces rather than being a 1M-only numerical artifact.
\end{enumerate}
